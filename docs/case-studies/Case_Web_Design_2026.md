## 2. The 4-Pillar Customer Discovery Framework

Extracted from Contentsquare, this is the mental model for mapping any page:

1. **What** do they want to find out? (Clarity of Hero/Value Prop)
2. **Where** do they want to go? (Navigation/Breadcrumbs)
3. **What** do they want to do? (CTAs/Interactions)
4. **What** do they want to buy/convert on? (Frictionless checkout/Contact)

## 3. High-Leverage De-Facto Advice (The "Steal")

### A. The "3-5 Second" Rule

Within 3 seconds of landing, a user must know:

- Who you are.
- What problem you solve.
- How to start.
*Athena Integration*: Every landing page managed by Athena must pass this "Blink Test."

### B. Navigation Conventions (Don't be Cute)

- **Place Menus** in expected locations (Top or Left corner).
- **Logo Behavior**: Always link the logo back to the Homepage.
- **Labels**: "About Us" > "Our Journey". "Contact" > "Get in Touch" (unless brand tone allows).
- **Sticky Elements**: Use sticky navigation or footers for long pages so users don't have to scroll up to convert.

### C. Readability Physics

- **Line Length**: Limit to 45-75 characters per line for optimal reading.
- **Chunking**: Use bullet points and short paragraphs.
- **Micro-Animations**: Use hover states on buttons (color change) to give instant feedback.
- **Font Scale**: Stick to 2-3 sizes max to maintain visual hierarchy.

### D. The "Let it Breathe" (White Space) Quotient

- Avoid crowding elements.
- Increase padding between sections to create a "Premium/Clean" feel.
- High white space = Reduced cognitive load = Higher trust.

---

## 4. Operational Checklist for 2026

- [ ] **Mobile-First Orientation**: Design for the thumb, not just the cursor.
- [ ] **Light/Dark Mode Symmetry**: Ensure contrast accessibility on both.
- [ ] **Fast Load/Lazy Load**: Compress images <500KB; use `.webp` and lazy loading.
- [ ] **Social Proof/Testimonials**: Succinct personal quotes with personality.
- [ ] **Feedback Loops**: Set up Analytics (Google/Squarespace) on Day 1 to track "Rage Clicks" or "Dead Ends."
